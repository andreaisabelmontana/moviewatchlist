Track every film — and get recommendations from your taste.

MovieWatchlist is a SQLite-backed Python library and CLI for tracking watched and to-watch films. Its content-based recommender vectorises each film's genres and tags with TF-IDF and ranks unseen films by cosine similarity to a taste profile built from what you've rated highly.

01 — How the recommender works

Content-based, from your ratings.

No accounts, no collaborative filtering — recommendations come entirely from the content of films you rated and the weight you gave them.

🏷️

1. Feature each film

Every film becomes a bag of content features: its genres plus descriptive tags (e.g. space, noir, coming-of-age).

📐

2. TF-IDF vectorise

Features are TF-IDF–weighted across the whole catalog, so ubiquitous genres are down-weighted and rarer, more telling tags carry more signal.

🎯

3. Build a taste profile

Watched films are summed, each weighted by rating − 6. High ratings pull toward their content; low ratings push away.

📊

4. Rank by similarity

Unseen films are scored by cosine similarity to the profile. Already-watched films are excluded.

🔖

5. Bias to watchlist

Films you've flagged to-watch get a small bonus so the recommender can resurface things you already planned.

🗄️

SQLite-backed

Catalog and lists persist in SQLite; the same store powers the CLI and the recommender.

02 — What it does

A film diary with a brain.

✅

Watched + rate

Mark films watched and rate them 0–10; the to_watch → watched transition carries the rating.

🔖

Watchlist

Queue films you want to watch and filter the catalog by genre and year.

📈

Stats

Genre breakdown, average rating, and total runtime over everything you've watched.

✨

Recommendations

Content-based suggestions from your taste profile, excluding what you've already seen.

⌨️

CLI

An argparse CLI: init, watch, towatch, list, stats, recommend.

🧪

Tested

20 pytest tests covering CRUD, status transitions, persistence, stats, and recommendation quality.

03 — In Python

The real engine.

The interactive demo above mirrors this exactly — same catalog, same scoring.

# load the catalog, rate some sci-fi highly, get recommendations
from watchlist import WatchlistStore, load_catalog_file
from watchlist.recommender import ContentRecommender

store = WatchlistStore("films.db")
load_catalog_file(store)

store.mark_watched("Interstellar", 2014, rating=9.5)
store.mark_watched("The Matrix", 1999, rating=9.0)
store.mark_watched("Arrival", 2016, rating=9.0)
store.add_to_watchlist("Dune", 2021)

for r in ContentRecommender(store).recommend(k=5):
    print(r.score, r.film.title)

# 0.416  Dune (2021)            — surfaced from the watchlist
# 0.382  Edge of Tomorrow (2014)
# 0.369  Blade Runner 2049 (2017)
# 0.340  Gravity (2013)
